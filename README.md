## 🚲 Project Overview
Cyclistic is a fictional bike-share company in Chicago. The director of marketing believes the company’s future success depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships. 

**The Business Task:** Analyze 12 months of historical trip data to understand how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently, and design a targeted marketing strategy to convert casual riders into profitable annual members.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack Used
* **Data Cleaning & Aggregation:** R (Tidyverse, Dplyr, Lubridate), Microsoft Excel
* **Data Visualization:** Tableau
* **Presentations & Reporting:** Canva, PowerPoint, Markdown

## 📊 Key Insights
1. **The Core User Base:** Annual members account for 64.2% of all trips, using the system primarily for routine weekday commuting.
2. **The Duration Gap:** Casual riders hold onto bikes for an average of 22.2 minutes (82% longer than members), indicating leisure and sightseeing usage.
3. **The Conversion Window:** Casual ridership explodes on the weekends and during the summer months (June-August), identifying the exact time and place to deploy marketing capital.

## 🎯 Strategic Recommendations
1. Launch a **"Weekend Explorer"** upgrade tier targeting Saturday/Sunday riders.
2. Deploy a **Summer Conversion Campaign** using geo-fenced digital ads at high-casual traffic stations (e.g., Navy Pier).
3. Market the **"Duration Value"** of an annual pass to casuals to offset the cost of long, 20+ minute leisure rides.
