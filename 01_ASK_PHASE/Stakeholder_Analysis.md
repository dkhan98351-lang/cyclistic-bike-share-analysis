# Stakeholder Analysis
## Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

---

## Stakeholder Identification & Priorities

Understanding stakeholder needs and decision-making criteria is critical for presenting findings effectively.

---

## Primary Stakeholder: Lily Moreno
**Title:** Director of Marketing  
**Your Relationship:** Direct Manager

### Priorities & Goals:
- **Maximize annual memberships** to drive company growth
- Design marketing strategies that convert casual riders to members
- Secure executive approval for new marketing initiatives
- Optimize marketing spend based on customer behavioral insights

### Decision-Making Criteria:
- Data must be compelling and professional
- Insights must be actionable and specific
- Recommendations should align with digital marketing capabilities
- Evidence should support proposed budget allocation

### Key Concerns:
- Current marketing approach lacks customer segmentation strategy
- Need to understand *why* casual riders haven't converted
- Budget constraints require efficient targeting
- Competitive pressure to innovate marketing tactics

### How to Engage:
- Present clear, data-driven narratives
- Link findings directly to marketing strategy implications
- Propose specific channels and messages based on usage patterns
- Quantify potential impact of recommendations

---

## Secondary Stakeholder: Cyclistic Executive Team
**Title:** Executive Leadership  
**Your Relationship:** Decision Approval Authority

### Priorities & Goals:
- Ensure company profitability and sustainable growth
- Allocate marketing budget efficiently
- Minimize business risk with data-backed decisions
- Maintain competitive market position

### Decision-Making Criteria:
- **Detail-oriented** (explicitly noted in case study)
- Require comprehensive, well-researched analysis
- Demand statistical rigor and methodological transparency
- Need clear ROI projections and risk assessments

### Key Concerns:
- Uncertainty about customer behavior segmentation
- Concerns about marketing effectiveness
- Budget justification for new initiatives
- Implementation feasibility and timeline

### How to Engage:
- Provide thorough methodology documentation
- Use professional, polished visualizations
- Include confidence levels and data limitations
- Present multiple scenarios where applicable
- Quantify expected outcomes and risks

## Customer Stakeholders

### Casual Riders
**Relationship:** Analysis Subjects  
**Characteristics:**
- Purchase single-ride or full-day passes (not annual members)
- Already aware of and use Cyclistic service
- Likely use bikes for leisure rather than commuting
- Cost-conscious (pay per ride rather than annual fee)

**Understanding These Stakeholders Helps:**
- Identify barriers to membership conversion
- Design messaging that addresses their needs
- Determine optimal pricing or value propositions
- Target digital media effectively

### Annual Members
**Relationship:** Analysis Comparison Group  
**Characteristics:**
- Committed to annual membership
- Likely use bikes for regular commuting
- See value in unlimited access
- Represent profitable revenue model

**Understanding These Stakeholders Helps:**
- Identify membership value drivers
- Understand retention factors
- Model behavior aspirations for casual riders
- Define product-market fit for members
