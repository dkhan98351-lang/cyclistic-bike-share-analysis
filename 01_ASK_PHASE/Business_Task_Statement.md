# Business Task Statement
## Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

---

## Executive Summary

**Primary Objective:**  
Understand how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently to design data-driven marketing strategies that convert casual riders into annual members.

---

## Business Context

**Company Overview:**
- **Cyclistic** is a Chicago-based bike-share company with 5,824 bicycles and 692 docking stations
- The fleet includes traditional bikes, reclining bikes, hand tricycles, and cargo bikes to serve diverse rider needs
- ~8% of riders use assistive options; ~30% use bikes for daily commutes
- The majority of riders use bikes for leisure purposes

**Business Situation:**
- Cyclistic's finance team has determined that **annual members are significantly more profitable** than casual riders
- Current marketing strategy focuses on general awareness and broad consumer appeal
- Pricing flexibility (single-ride passes, full-day passes, annual memberships) has helped attract customers but hasn't maximized member conversion

---

## The Problem We're Solving

**Challenge:**  
Despite general market awareness, Cyclistic needs to optimize its marketing approach to convert existing casual riders—who are already familiar with the service and have chosen Cyclistic for their mobility needs—into annual members.

**Why This Matters:**
- Maximizing annual memberships is critical to future company growth
- Casual riders represent an opportunity market with established brand awareness
- Competitive marketing requires understanding behavioral differences between customer segments

---

## Key Business Question

**Primary Research Question:**  
**How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?**

This question will form the foundation for answering two secondary business questions:
- Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
- How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

---

## Assigned Responsibility

**Role:** Junior Data Analyst, Marketing Analytics Team  
**Manager:** Lily Moreno, Director of Marketing  
**Target Audience:** Cyclistic Executive Team (detail-oriented decision-makers)

---

## Expected Outcomes & Impact

Upon completion of this analysis, findings will:
1. Identify key behavioral differences between member types
2. Reveal usage patterns that drive membership value
3. Support evidence-based marketing recommendations
4. Enable executive approval of a new, targeted marketing strategy
5. Guide digital media allocation decisions

---

## Constraints & Considerations

- **Data Privacy:** Personal identifiable information (PII) cannot be used; ride-level data only
- **Scope:** Analysis focuses on historical trip data patterns
- **Timeline:** One-week project completion goal
- **Tools:** Excel, SQL, or R for data analysis; visualization software for presentation

---

## Success Criteria

This analysis will be successful when:
✓ Clear patterns between member and casual rider behavior are identified  
✓ Insights are backed by quantitative analysis and professional visualizations  
✓ Recommendations are actionable and supported by data evidence  
✓ Executive team has sufficient confidence to approve new marketing initiatives
