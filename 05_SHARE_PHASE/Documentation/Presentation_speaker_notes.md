# Executive Presentation: Speaker Notes
## Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

**Presenter:** Danish Khan
**Audience:** Lily Moreno (Director of Marketing) & Cyclistic Executive Team
**Duration Goal:** ~10-15 Minutes

---

**Slide 1: Title Slide**
* *"Good morning, everyone. My name is Danish Khan, and I'm a Junior Data Analyst here on the Marketing Analytics team. Today, I'm excited to present our data-driven strategy for Cyclistic's next phase of growth: converting our casual riders into profitable annual members."*

**Slide 2: The Business Challenge**
* *"We know from our finance team that annual members drive our profitability. Our objective was simple: to figure out exactly how our casual riders differ from our members, so we can target them with precision."*

**Slide 3 & 4: Ask Phase (Stakeholders)**
* *"Before touching the data, we defined what success looks like for this room. For Lily, that means actionable marketing insights. For the Executive team, that means statistical rigor and data privacy compliance. We ensured no PII (Personally Identifiable Information) was used in this analysis to protect our users."*

**Slide 5 & 6: Prepare & Process Phase**
* *"We didn't just look at a sample. We pulled a full 12 months of historical data—over 3.7 million rides. Because of the sheer volume, I processed the data file-by-file in Excel to ensure integrity, and then merged it using R scripts to handle the scale. We filtered out system errors and extreme outliers to ensure the insights you see today are 100% reliable."*

**Slide 7: Overall Ridership**
* *"Let's look at the baseline. Our system is healthy. Members make up the stable core at 64% of volume. But that 36% Casual slice represents 1.3 million rides—that is our immediate conversion opportunity."*

**Slide 8: Ride Duration**
* *"This is our first major behavioral difference. When we look at duration, Casual trips are 82% longer. Members use the bikes for quick, 12-minute point-to-point utility. Casual riders are holding the bikes for an average of 22 minutes. We aren't just selling them transportation; we are selling them an experience."*

**Slide 9: Day of the Week**
* *"This chart is perhaps the most revealing. Notice the deep blue Member line—it's consistent Monday through Friday. These are our commuters. Now look at the red Casual line. It forms a massive mountain on the weekends. Over a third of all casual riding happens on Saturday and Sunday."*

**Slide 10: Seasonal Trends**
* *"Zooming out to the full year, we see the 'Summer Spike.' During the winter, the system is almost entirely commuters. But in June, July, and August, casual ridership explodes, accounting for over 43% of all trips. This is our prime marketing window."*

**Slide 11: Geographic Preferences**
* *"Finally, we asked: where are they riding? Members cluster inland near business districts and transit hubs like Kingsbury and Kinzie. Casual riders heavily cluster along the lakefront and at tourist attractions like Navy Pier."*

**Slide 12: Strategic Recommendations**
* *"Based on these validated behaviors, I have three recommendations for our next marketing sprint:*
  1. *First, the **Weekend Explorer**: Let's launch a 'Weekend-to-Annual' upgrade promotion targeting those Saturday/Sunday peaks.*
  2. *Second, the **Summer Takeover**: We should allocate the bulk of our digital ad budget to June-August, specifically geo-fencing ads around Navy Pier and Lake Shore Drive.*
  3. *Third, **Cost-Value Messaging**: Because casuals ride 82% longer, we should emphasize how an annual pass saves them money on those long, 20+ minute leisure rides."*

**Slide 13: Conclusion**
* *"By aligning our marketing with their natural weekend and summer behaviors, we can efficiently convert these riders. Thank you for your time, I'd love to open the floor to any questions about the data or our methodology."*
